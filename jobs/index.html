Sales Representative Vacancy

Gitlab is quickly becoming more popular amongst organisations throughout the world. To keep up with enquiries, Gitlab.com is looking for a Sales Representative (part-time or fulltime), who will be the first contact for organisations considering using Gitlab, or purchasing our consultancy or subscription services. If you are interested in turning such enquiries into meaningful and long-lasting customer relationships, and helping organisations manage their source code, we would love to meet you.

You would be responding to all non-technical enquiries that reach us. You will have lots of different responsibilities, and freedom to shape our commercial processes. Your contact with (potential) customers can be in person, via email and via telephone. When you are not responding to enquiries, you will be actively promoting Gitlab.com and our services.

You need to be based in the United States, and you need to have an interest in version management. Ideally, You need to be familiar with software development terminology. At Gitlab.com, we mostly work from home and love to go to Rails/Ruby conferences (paid by Gitlab.com). We are looking for people who can handle and enjoy the freedom that comes with working from home. Our water coolers are the Gitlab core team and Gitlab contributors Campfire chat rooms, plus a daily Hangout.

Interested in joining our motivated team? Send us a short motivation plus your resume or a link to your LinkedIn profile at support@gitlab.com.

Support engineer vacancy

GitLab.com is growing quickly and we would love to grow our team with a support engineer. Although there are many people that maintain their GitLab installation themselves the rising popularity of GitLab means that our professional services are in high demand. If you got the skills to help to upgrade and maintain GitLab we would love to talk to you.

You would be engaging with our customers, this can be anything from as small advertising firm, a university to Fortune 100 clients. You have appointments thoughout the day with clients that have filled out a small questionnarry. You get remote access to their GitLab server(s) and diagnose, upgrade, troubleshoot, configure, backup and reinstall it. During the the appointment you keep in touch with the client trough chat or video conference.

When you do not have an appointment you engage with the rest of the core team to improve GitLab. You fix probles, add features, improve documentation and engage with people on our feedback forum.

You need to have experience with Unix and can program. Most clients will run RedHat or Debian variants and GitLab is written in Ruby on Rails. But we are open to people who for example love Solaris and C, as long as you like Unix and programming you will fit in. You don't have to be an English native but good written English and adequate verbal English is a must to engage with clients.

At GitLab.com we mostly work from home and love to go to Rails conferences (paid for by GitLab.com). We are looking for people who can handle and enjoy the freedom that comes with working from home. Our watercoolers are the GitLab core team and GitLab contributors Campfire chat rooms and a daily Hangout.

Interested? Please send an email to support@gitlab.com with a resume or a link to your LinkedIn profile.
