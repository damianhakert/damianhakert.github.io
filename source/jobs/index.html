Director of Sales San Francisco

Gitlab B.V. is quickly becoming more popular amongst organisations throughout the world to manage their source code. To keep up with our growth Gitlab B.V. is looking for a Director of Sales to act as our sales lead. This position will be responsible for Gitlab subscription deals for Fortune 500 companies and large governmental organizations. With tremendous inbound interest from these organizations, you will need to lead the sales process.

Responsibilities:

  • Lead our account managers
  • Get directly involved in deals when necessary
  • Post-mortem analysis on wins/losses
  • Manage and report on sales pipeline
  • Provide input on product direction and strategy as a result of sales performance, post mortems and competitive analysis.
  • Help marketing with developing product/content marketing materials (blog posts, white papers, product briefs, slide decks) that reflect GitLab's core value proposition, competitive advantage and market positioning

Requirements:

  • B2B sales experience
  • Experience closing large deals ($100k+)
  • Experience selling open source software (RedHat, MySQL, etc.) - ideally in the United States

Why work for Gitlab?

Large performance bonus and/or stake in the company. If you have ambition, there is a growth parth towards becoming a VP of Sales. You will be working in our San Francisco office.

Interaction Engineer Remote

GitLab is looking for someone that can make GitLab look and feel even better. We recognize from the success of Apple and Slack that design and usability is as important as solid engineering. As interaction engineer, you will be responsible for setting a higher standard in usability and design in GitLab.

We're looking for someone that can create mockups quickly and implement these themselves. You have to have an eye for clean design and understand what makes an application easy to use and feel good.

Employees at Gitlab work independently and you will have to be responsible for coming up with your own improvements as well as for their full implementation, start to finish. In addition, we often work on a consultancy basis for customers and this position requires the ability to collaborate with other engineers to implement feeatures on request.

GitLab is a booming startup, meaning there is much more work than hands available. As first interaction engineer, everything you do will have a major impact on the experience of everyone that uses GitLab: hundreds of thousands of organizations.

We're a distributed company and much of your work will be done remotely. We welcome candidates from all countries between Pacific time and Eastern European time (North America, South America, Europe and Africa). Most of our customers are in the continental U.S. time zones. We're considering opening an office in San Francisco in the future.

Responsibilities:

  • Improve screens
  • Work with developers to improve flows
  • Conduct user testing
  • Create wireframes/mockups for new features

Requirements:

  • Familiar with HTML, CSS and JS.
  • jQuery. Note: we don't use many JS libraries or frameworks, but we welcome any experience in one (Ember, Angular, etc). GitLab is built on Bootstrap 3.
